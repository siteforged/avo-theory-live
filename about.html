Our Story

From a folding table at the Saturday market to a full-service kitchen — here's how Avo Theory came to be, and why we do what we do.

👩‍🍳
2018
Founded Austin, TX
Where It Began

A Saturday obsession that became a restaurant

In 2018, Maya Rivera and Jake Okonkwo were two food-obsessed friends with a shared belief: the avocado was chronically under-respected. Not as a trend, not as a hashtag — but as a genuinely complex, nourishing, endlessly versatile fruit deserving a real kitchen.

They started with a single rented tent at the Austin Downtown Farmers' Market, offering five simple guac preparations and hand-pressed totopos. Within six weeks, the wait list was 40 people long. Within six months, they had a space.

Today, Avo Theory occupies a 3,400 sq-ft light-filled dining room on Green Leaf Blvd, serves over 500 guests per day, and still buys its avocados from the same three family farms it started with.

Our Journey

Milestones

  1. April 2018

    Farmers' Market Launch

    Maya and Jake debut their guac stand to instant local acclaim.

  2. January 2019

    Brick-and-Mortar Opens

    412 Green Leaf Blvd opens its doors — a 40-seat café with a full kitchen.

  3. June 2020

    Best Brunch Spot — Austin Eater

    Named "Best Brunch Spot in Austin" by Eater and Texas Monthly in the same month.

  4. March 2021

    Expansion to 100 Seats

    We tripled our dining room and added a dedicated brunch bar and events space.

  5. 2022 – 2023

    Catering & Zero-Waste Commitment

    Launched full-service catering and achieved 98% waste diversion via composting & donation programs.

  6. Today

    120+ Items, 6k+ Avos Per Week

    Still independently owned, still sourcing from the same farms, still obsessed with the perfect avocado.

What Drives Us

Our Values

These aren't slogans on a wall. They're the decisions we make every single day in the kitchen.

  • 🌱

    Sourced with Care

    We buy from farms we know by name. Our avocados travel under 400 miles to reach your plate.

  • ♻️

    Zero Waste Kitchen

    Avocado pits become dye. Skins go to compost. We redirect 98% of kitchen waste from landfill.

  • 🤝

    Community First

    We donate surplus meals daily to local shelters and sponsor Austin youth cooking education programs.

  • 🍽️

    Made to Order

    Nothing sits under a heat lamp. Every dish is made fresh when you order — no compromises.

  • 🧑‍🤝‍🧑

    Inclusive Table

    Vegan, gluten-free, allergen-aware — we design menus so every guest eats something delicious.

  • 💚

    Fair Wages Always

    Every team member earns a living wage. We believe great food starts with a respected team.
